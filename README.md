## Background

Text-to-speech (TTS) is an important assistive technology for people who have lost their voice due to cancer, or just for people who need to rest their voice (teachers or performers). TTS applications for everyday use need to be intuitive and fast to maintain conversation flow. Instruction-guided TTS uses a natural-language style prompt (e.g., "The man spoke softly with an American accent at a slow pace.") to control the manner of speech generated by the speech synthesis system. This work proposes a mechanism, called ConvoStyleGraph, to automatically suggest a style prompt based on the dialogue context, enabling users to maintain a natural conversation flow even with an assitive TTS app.

## Methodology (Tentative)

ConvoStyleGraph will represent the turns in conversation as nodes in a graph, which are connected based on relevance. The presence of both audio and textual modalities will incorporate rich prosodic and semantic information to inform the style of speech in the conversation so far. 

A final embedding will be produced from this graph, then this will be passed through a transformer mapping layer which projects the dialogue into the word embedding space of an LLM. The final embedding will be used as a learned prefix vector to condition the LLM to produce style prompt suggestions.
